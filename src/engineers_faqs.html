Building data security mechanisms in-house is expensive and time-consuming because there's no standard for application-layer data security. Not enough data security engineers in the world meet the demand. Data Security tools available in the marketplace today are just tools and bring with them an implicit expectation that you will build a bespoke solution.

Peacemakr is a system that delivers key material across a complex ecosystem of software and helps that software use those keys correctly to encrypt and decrypt data. This allows your software to integrate end-to-end encryption into any application, quickly.

SSL/TLS only protects data when it is in transit. Encryption at rest only protects data when it is on disk. Both of these leave data exposed everywhere else. This allows threats many chances to steal the data from databases, applications, in logs, proxies, or just by reading the disk.

End-to-End-Encryption inside your application protects the data as soon as it’s created. Your data’s entire lifecycle is now protected, eliminating unessesary risks to data leaks.

For example, consider a log collection system that transmits data to a log aggregator. If you only encrypt with TLS, then the logs are exposed on both the original system and in the aggregator. However, by encrypting the logs as soon as they are produced with Peacemakr’s End-to-End-Encryption, then the logs are protected in all systems. The Application-Layer Encryption enabled customers to use a SaaS provider to aggregate logs without having to trust a multi-tenant cloud.

The security of your encrypted data depends on the security and management of your keys. Building a Key Lifecycle Management and Secure and Scalable Key Delivery mechanisms are not trivial engineering tasks. Further, there’s no standard to help guide what the design should be. This not only increases the likelihood of costly mistakes, but it prohibits interoperability between different systems across the different implementations, which restricts the ability to securely collaborate with future vendors, partners, and customers.

Keys come from your Key Derivers. Everyone starts with a Key Deriver in the cloud, but you can run these modular components wherever you need.

The Key Deriver is what produces all your symmetric keys. It’s a software package that you can run on any Unix platform.

It depend on your threat model. When your Key Deriver is self-hosted, your keys are protected against the same threat models as protect your Key Deriver.

In order to make things easy, we host the first Key Deriver for you in the cloud. If you need to bring your Key Deriver in-house you have the option to relocate your Key Deriver. You can do this relocation over and over; in fact, you can do it any time you rotate your keys.

A client is anything software that integrates with and registers an SDK. Once a key is derived it’s encrypted with the client's public asymmetric key and delivered to that specific authorized client. Not even Peacemakr can snoop on that key material. For the strongest zero-trust threat model, you'd need to whitelist your trusted Certificate Authority and sign your clients and Key Derivers public keys (yes, we have an API for that).

Peacemakr’s network provides the basis for secure collaboration.

At Peacemakr, we divide our customers into Organizations. Your account belongs to an Organization that defines an authorization scope for your keys. You can share some of the keys in your Organization with any Organization in the Peacemakr network. Even when your authorized a collaborator, you control your keys and security policies, even in someone else's system. End a collaboration at any time, and their access to your keys are revoked.
